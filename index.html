Typography is inherently rooted in a systematic set of guidelines. As Willi Kunz puts it, these systems act as a ‘moral code’ for the alphabet and enable characters to work together, by regulating and defining their appearance.

.modular (dot-modular) is a collaborative, crowd-sourced typeface that attempts to explore how these systematic rules can also allow for a dynamic, ever changing set of letterforms. It relies on a classic and simple modular grid with many, many possibilities for letterforms to be drawn within it.

.modular also takes a hands-off approach by using the participant’s nose as a brush with the screen being the canvas. Our hands are used to drawing letterforms everyday, but how does our understanding of each shape, line and curve change when we remove that finger-based instinctual movement?
